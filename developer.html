About

I'm an independent developer who builds software because I want it to exist. Spyglass started as a personal project — a Minecraft companion app I wished I had while playing. It grew from a simple block reference into a full-featured toolkit with live world integration, 19 calculators, and a desktop companion that streams your save data to your phone.

I believe good tools should be fast, offline-capable, and respect your privacy. No accounts required, no cloud dependency, no tracking beyond what you opt into. Everything runs locally.

Projects

Spyglass

Android companion app for Minecraft Java Edition. Browse blocks, items, mobs, biomes, enchantments, potions, recipes, trades, structures, advancements, and commands. 19 built-in calculators. Fully offline with automatic data sync.

Kotlin Jetpack Compose Android

Spyglass Connect

Desktop companion app that reads your Minecraft Java Edition save files and streams data to your phone over local WiFi. View inventory, search chests, locate structures, and explore an overhead map — all encrypted end-to-end.

Kotlin Compose Multiplatform Desktop

Spyglass Data

Public repository of Minecraft reference data. 1,167 blocks, 502 items, 1,291 recipes, and more — all hand-validated against the Minecraft Wiki. Powers the automatic sync in Spyglass.

JSON Open Data

Philosophy

  • Privacy first. No accounts. No cloud. Local data, local network. You choose what to share.
  • Offline by default. Everything works without internet. Sync is optional and incremental.
  • One developer, no compromises. I build what I want, the way I want. No investors, no product managers, no dark patterns.
